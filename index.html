Our Story

We are so happy you are part of this chapter.

Lucy and Cosmin met in the summer of 2022, when Lucy was finishing a project for her master’s degree in Bucharest. Lucy was on the train back from a weekend in Bulgaria, when Cosmin happened to be sitting in her seat. After a train ride home together, they exchanged contact information, and what started as seeing each other in friend group settings, soon became planning dates for just the two of them. They spent the rest of that summer inseparable, and when Lucy went back home at the end of the summer, Cosmin promised to visit her in the United States.

True to his word, Cosmin visited in October, which also happened to be his first time in the United States. His first trip to the U.S. included exploring Little Rock, celebrating Halloween, and even catching a Mavericks–Jazz game in Dallas.

After a year of long distance, Lucy moved to Bucharest when she graduated from her master’s program.

They spent the next two years living in Bucharest, discovering new favorite restaurants, going to the movies, taking weekend excursions, and visiting family in Eforie Sud and in the United States.

When Lucy’s job brought her back to the United States, they planned a post-holiday trip to Paris — and in the gardens at Versailles, Cosmin asked Lucy to marry him.

FAQs

What is the best way to get to Bucharest?

Bucharest Henri Coandă International Airport (OTP) is the main airport to fly into.

What is the dress code?

This will be a formal event, so the dress code will be formal. It will be very hot, so plan for suits/dresses/formal wear that will be comfortable!

Will the wedding be indoors or outdoors?

Most everything will take place indoors!

The religious ceremony and main reception will be inside, with cocktail hour being outside on a terrace, weather permitting.

What if I have a dietary restriction?

When you RSVP you will have the option to mark any dietary restrictions you might have. We will do our best to accommodate all diets!

What is the best way to get around Bucharest?

We will provide a shuttle for transportation between the religious ceremony and reception.

For airport pick-up or drop-off, your hotel can arrange a shuttle or private transfer—the hotel where we reserved a room block can help coordinate this.

Uber is also an easy option and what we usually use to get to and from the airport.

There is a train that connects the airport to Bucharest’s main station, Gara de Nord, but it’s reportedly slow and I haven’t tried it myself.

Uber is easy, reliable, and inexpensive for short trips around the city. It is best to have the app already downloaded and your payment already set up before you arrive in Bucharest.

Bucharest’s subway system is clean and efficient (operating 5:00 AM–11:00 PM) — most wedding activities are near Piața Romană or Piața Universității. See the metro map here: visitbucharest.today/bucharest-metro

The street that most of the wedding activities is set on, Calea Victoriei, is a very walkable area - you can easily walk to sites such as Old Town, Cismigiu Park, and the Royal Palace, along with many cute stores, cafes, and restaurants.

If you are planning on visiting sites outside of Bucharest, you can take the train from the main station, Gara De Nord. To purchase train tickets, please use this website: cfrcalatori.ro

Are children allowed?

If you have any children they should already be accounted for when you RSVP!

What is the Romanian Currency/ Language

The Romanian currency is a leu.

1 dollar = 4.40 Lei

Many places do accept card payments, but it is advised to have some cash on hand.

The spoken language is Romanian.

You can easily get around with English in the city; less so the farther out of the city you venture.

Any other tips and tricks?

Please keep in mind that this will be a very hot time of the year to be traveling in Europe/Romania. Many older buildings and trains do not have A/C, so I suggest having a hand fan/mini electronic fan.

"Apa plata" is what to ask for if you want "flat" water (non sparkling water).

Romanian food heavily consist of meat and pork, but Bucharest does offer many international restaurants that should accommodate dietary restrictions.

Please reach out to us if you do have any specific questions or concerns!! We are happy to help you prepare for such a big international journey.
